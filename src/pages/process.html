Phase 1

Sourcing

We study each plant individually and build direct relationships with certified organic farmers in India.

01

Understanding the Ingredient

We study each plant individually - its ideal soil, climate, and growing conditions - before sourcing begins.

  • Scientific and nutritional data analysis
  • Agronomic research and botanical studies
  • Soil composition, climate, and seasonal cycle assessment
02

Finding Farms That Share Our Standards

We work directly with certified organic farmers in India, building long-term relationships based on trust.

  • On-site visits by our team members
  • Direct farmer relationships
  • Years of collaboration development
Phase 2

Testing & Verification

Every batch is held in quarantine until all testing is complete. No material enters production without verification.

03

Receipt & Inspection

Every delivery is checked on arrival - documents, certifications, and transport conditions - before anything is unloaded.

  • Document verification and certification check
  • Transport condition assessment
  • Visual inspection of packaging integrity
04

Storing What We've Sourced

Organic materials are stored in a dedicated warehouse with strict separation, clean pallets, and FIFO rotation.

  • Dedicated organic storage area
  • Temperature and humidity monitoring
  • FIFO inventory rotation
05

Mandatory Quarantine

Every batch is held under a blue "QC HOLD" label. No material enters production until all testing is complete.

06

Raw Material Sampling

Our QA/QC team takes samples from multiple bags using dedicated tools. Every detail is logged.

07

Internal Quality Testing

Visual inspection, botanical identity, moisture content, and particle size are checked in-house first.

  • Visual and organoleptic inspection
  • Botanical identity verification
  • Moisture and particle size analysis
08

External Laboratory Testing

Independent labs test for heavy metals, microbiologicals, pesticide residues, and regulatory compliance.

  • Heavy metals panel (Pb, Cd, As, Hg)
  • Microbiological screening
  • Pesticide residue analysis
09

Released or Rejected

One final decision per batch. Compliant batches are released. Non-compliant batches are returned or destroyed.

Phase 3

Processing & Production

From cleaning to encapsulation, every step preserves ingredient integrity.

10

Cleaning & Material Prep

Fresh materials washed, dry materials inspected

11

Primary Drying

Vacuum tray drying at low temperatures

12

Size Reduction

Cut into uniform 1-3 cm pieces

13

Pre-Grinding

Initial size reduction for processing

14

Extraction

Where applicable for concentrated formulas

15

Final Drying

Precise moisture control

16

Fine Milling

Consistent particle size

17

Sieving

80-mesh screen filtration

18

Blending

Precise formula composition

19

Encapsulation

Continuous weight monitoring

Phase 4

Packaging & Release

Every capsule is counted, sealed, and made fully traceable.

20

Counting & Filling

High-accuracy sensors count capsules into bottles. Every count verified.

  • Automated capsule counting
  • Weight verification per bottle
  • Visual quality inspection
21

Sealing & Labeling

Tamper-evident seals applied. Labels include batch number and expiration.

  • Tamper-evident seal application
  • Batch number and expiration printing
  • Label accuracy verification
22

Traceability & Batch Release

Every capsule is fully traceable from farm to finished bottle with complete documentation.

  • Complete batch documentation
  • Farm-to-bottle traceability
  • Quality release authorization
