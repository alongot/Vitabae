Safety Information

Generally Well Tolerated

Most people experience no side effects when used as directed.

Mild Effects (Uncommon)

  • Mild digestive discomfort
  • Heartburn
  • Mild nausea (rare)

Critical Warning

Ginger may increase the risk of bleeding when taken with anticoagulant or antiplatelet medications. Consult your healthcare provider before use if you take blood thinners or have a bleeding disorder.

Not Suitable For

  • Individuals under 18 years of age
  • Those with bleeding disorders
  • Those with gallstones
  • Pregnant women without medical supervision
  • Those taking anticoagulant medications without medical guidance

Frequently Asked Questions

Ginger may be used during pregnancy for occasional digestive support, but only under medical supervision and for short-term use. Always consult your healthcare provider before taking any supplement during pregnancy.

For digestive support, many people notice benefits within 1-2 weeks of consistent use. For menstrual comfort, 4-6 weeks of regular use may be needed. Individual results vary.

Ginger is generally compatible with most supplements. However, use caution when combining with supplements that may affect bleeding (fish oil, vitamin E). Always inform your healthcare provider about all supplements you take.
